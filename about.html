About Me

Background

I was born in Huntington, New York, and I've lived there all my life until coming to Pittsburgh for school at Carnegie Mellon University. I grew up playing video games, and was introduced to programming when command blocks were added to Minecraft. Despite the fairly simple and limited scope, I fell in love with writing code that could have a noticeable effect on my gameplay.

I eventually started modding games such as Cave Story and Minecraft as I learned basic programming concepts in school. My district didn't have any computer science courses at the time, but some older students (mainly in the robotics club) taught me enough to get started.

I continued programming for my middle and high school robotics teams, using visual scripting for lego robots with the former and Java for the latter. Academically, I was mainly focused on math. Once I came to CMU, though, I found a new appreciation for computer science, especially in the contexts of 3D software, computer graphics, and game programming. Joining CMU's Game Creation Society has furthered these interests.
