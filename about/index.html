About Me

I'm Andrew, a senior at Carnegie Mellon University studying discrete math and game design. I came to school wanting to do math research for some reason, then decided to focus more on computer science because I enjoyed modding games like Minecraft.

I decided to try out the Godot game engine one time because I was fed up with insufficient documentation for modding Call of Duty, and I reasoned that I would rather make my own games with insufficient documentation than try to mess with someone else's.

A friend eventually convinced me to join my school's Game Creation Society, where I really fell in love with game development. I learned to use Unity and Unreal, and branched out into building for XR. Now making games is my main interest.

Education

At CMU, I've studied a variety of mathematical topics such as linear algebra, combinatorics, set theory, and calculus, as well as various theoretical CS courses on data structures, algorithms, and systems.

I wanted to take the Computer Game Programming course, which required studying Computer Graphics first. These ended up being my favorite two classes I've taken at CMU and are the reason I've started focusing more on graphics for the last year or so (my final project in Game Programming in particular was very influential).

I like to play up my disinterest in math as a joke, since it's my major. It's true that as school has gone on I've wanted to focus more on CS and game courses and less on math ones, but that doesn't mean I'm not still interested in the math I'm doing all the time in my life. Besides the fun math in 3D graphics, I actually returned to discrete math research this past year when a friend approached me with a simple yet elegantly-structured problem (paper here).
